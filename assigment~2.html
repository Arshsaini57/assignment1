What are protective areas?

   It is a precisely defined geographic area that is acknowledged, devoted, and controlled either legally or via other practical means by the government authorities in order to accomplish the long-term preservation of nature.

Human activity, mostly for food production and forestry, is transforming landscapes. Today, one third of all land is degraded or degrading, which harms biodiversity and jeopardises essential ecosystem services such as carbon storage. Protected areas offer a solution. If effectively managed and fairly governed, such areas can safeguard nature and cultural resources, protect human health and well-being, provide sustainable livelihoods and so support sustainable development.
Why are protected areas important?
All around the world, protected areas serve a variety of purposes. In addition to providing crucial ecosystem services like preserving soils and watersheds and safeguarding human populations from natural disasters, they are crucial for maintaining biodiversity. Local communities place great importance on several protected sites. These locations uplift human spirits and stimulate the senses, giving them a sense of calm in a busy world. Important cultural values are embodied in protected landscapes, some showing examples of sustainable land use. Their significance extends to research and education, and they substantially contribute to the local and regional economy, mostly through tourism and recreation.
